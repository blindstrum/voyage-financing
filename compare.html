Market Commentary

Mortgage rates have stabilized in the mid-6% range after the Fed's recent pause. Borrowers with strong credit profiles are seeing the most competitive offers, especially from online-first lenders that carry lower overhead. The spread between the best and worst rates remains wide — over 0.70% — making comparison shopping essential.

What Moves Rates

Mortgage rates track the 10-year Treasury yield, but individual lender pricing depends on their margin, overhead, and appetite for new loans. Factors like your credit score, down payment, loan amount, and property type all affect the rate you'll be offered. Shopping multiple lenders can save you tens of thousands over a 30-year loan.
